Cyclistic Bike-Share Analysis

by Kenny Ngo  ·  Tools: R, Tableau Public  ·  Data: Jul 2025 – Jun 2026  ·  5,842,778 rows

1. Business Task

The goal of this analysis is to determine how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently. These insights will help the marketing team design a strategy to convert casual riders into annual members, which Cyclistic's finance team has identified as key to future growth.

2. Data Sources

12 months of Cyclistic historical trip data (July 2025 – June 2026) provided by Motivate International Inc. under a public license. Data includes ride id, bike type, start/end timestamps, station names, coordinates, and rider type (casual or member). Personally identifiable information is excluded due to privacy restrictions.

3. Data Cleaning

  • Loaded 12 CSV files into RStudio and combined into a single dataframe of 5,848,703 rows
  • Added ride_length column (difference between ended_at and started_at in minutes)
  • Added day_of_week column using wday() from lubridate
  • Added month and year columns for seasonal analysis
  • Removed 5,925 rows with zero or negative ride lengths
  • Removed rows with missing end coordinates
  • Final clean dataset: 5,842,778 rows

4. Analysis Summary

Ride Length: Casual riders averaged 18.9 minutes per ride vs 12.1 minutes for members. Casual riders take rides that are roughly 56% longer.

Day of Week: Casual riders peak on weekends (Saturday/Sunday), while members peak on weekdays (Tuesday–Thursday). This suggests casual riders use bikes for leisure and members use them for commuting.

Monthly Trends: Both groups peak in summer (June–August). Casual ridership drops off sharply in winter while members ride more consistently year-round.

Bike Type: Both groups prefer electric bikes over classic bikes. Members take significantly more total rides overall.

6. Top 3 Recommendations

1. Launch a Weekend-to-Membership Conversion Campaign

Casual riders are most active on weekends. Target them with promotions at docking stations and in the app on Friday evenings and Saturdays. Offer a discounted first month of membership to encourage sign-ups at their peak usage times.

2. Create a Summer Membership Promotion

Casual ridership spikes in summer. Launch a limited-time summer membership offer in May before the peak season. Highlight the cost savings of a membership versus paying per ride over a full summer.

3. Promote the Commuter Benefits of Membership

Create a digital campaign showing the cost comparison between casual rides and an annual membership for someone who rides a few times per week. Target through social media and email in spring when ridership starts picking up.
